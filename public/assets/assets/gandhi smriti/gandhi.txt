Gandhi Smriti: The Sacred Memorial of Mahatma Gandhi 🕊️
A Place of Reflection & Inspiration
Gandhi Smriti, formerly known as Birla House, is one of the most significant memorials dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi. Located on Tees January Road, Delhi, it is the place where Gandhi spent the last 144 days of his life before his assassination on January 30, 1948.

Now converted into a museum and memorial, it stands as a tribute to his life, principles, and immense contribution to India's independence. It offers visitors a deep and emotional connection to the Father of the Nation, making it a must-visit for history lovers and those seeking inspiration.

Major Attractions & Exhibits
🕊️ Martyr’s Column – The exact spot where Gandhi was assassinated while walking to his evening prayer.
🕊️ Gandhi’s Room – Preserved as it was, with his simple belongings, including a mattress, walking stick, and spinning wheel (charkha).
🕊️ The Footsteps Trail – Marks Gandhi’s final steps before he was shot by Nathuram Godse.
🕊️ Eternal Gandhi Multimedia Museum – Features rare photographs, letters, and interactive digital displays about his life and principles.
🕊️ Prayer Ground – The area where Gandhi held daily prayers, now a peaceful spot for visitors.
🕊️ Inspirational Quotes & Films – A collection of Gandhi’s teachings, speeches, and documentary screenings about his journey.

Why Visit Gandhi Smriti?
⭐ Experience History Firsthand – Stand at the very place where India’s most iconic leader spent his final moments.
⭐ Learn About Gandhi’s Life – See his personal belongings, letters, and rare photographs.
⭐ Peaceful & Thought-Provoking Atmosphere – A place for introspection and learning.
⭐ Free Entry & Informative Exhibits – A great way to understand India's independence movement.

How to Visit Gandhi Smriti
📍 Location: 5, Tees January Marg, Near India Gate, New Delhi
🕰 Timings: 10 AM – 5 PM (Closed on Mondays)
🎟 Entry Fees: Free for all visitors
🚇 Nearest Metro: Lok Kalyan Marg (Yellow Line) or Khan Market (Violet Line)
📸 Best Time to Visit: Morning or evening for a peaceful experience.

🚨 Visitor Tips:

Maintain silence and respect the memorial.
Photography is allowed, but some areas may have restrictions.
Spend time reading Gandhi’s quotes and watching the documentary films.
